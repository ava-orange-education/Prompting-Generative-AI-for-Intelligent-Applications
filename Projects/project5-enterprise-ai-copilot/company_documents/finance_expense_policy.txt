Finance Expense Policy

Purpose
This policy outlines the rules and procedures for submitting business expenses. All employees must follow these guidelines when requesting reimbursement for work-related costs. The goal is to ensure transparency, accountability, and fair use of company funds.

Eligible Expenses
The following categories are eligible for reimbursement: business travel including flights, hotels, and ground transportation; meals during business travel or client meetings; office supplies not available through the standard procurement process; software subscriptions approved by your manager; conference and training registration fees; and internet costs for approved remote workers.

Expense Limits
Daily meal expenses during business travel must not exceed 75 dollars per day. Hotel costs must stay within the approved rate for the destination city. A list of approved hotel rates by city is available on the finance portal. Flights should be booked at economy class for domestic travel. Business class is permitted for international flights over 6 hours with manager approval.

Submission Process
All expenses must be submitted through the company expense portal within 15 days of the expense date. Each submission must include a clear description, the business purpose, and a receipt or invoice. Submissions without receipts will not be processed. The finance team reviews submissions within 5 business days.

Approval Workflow
Expenses under 200 dollars require manager approval only. Expenses between 200 and 1,000 dollars require both manager and department head approval. Expenses over 1,000 dollars require additional approval from the Finance Director. Recurring expenses such as software subscriptions must be approved annually.

Reimbursement Timeline
Approved expenses are reimbursed in the next payroll cycle. If the expense is submitted after the payroll cutoff date, reimbursement will be included in the following cycle. Direct deposit is the standard reimbursement method. Employees who have not set up direct deposit should contact payroll@example.com.

Non-Eligible Expenses
The following are not eligible for reimbursement: personal travel expenses, alcohol, entertainment not related to business, fines or penalties, personal phone bills unless a remote work agreement is in place, and gifts to colleagues. If you are unsure whether an expense qualifies, contact the finance team before purchasing.

Corporate Credit Cards
Senior managers and frequent travelers may apply for a corporate credit card. Applications are reviewed by the Finance Director. Corporate card transactions must be reconciled monthly through the expense portal. Late reconciliation for two consecutive months may result in card suspension.

Auditing and Compliance
The finance team conducts quarterly audits of expense submissions. Employees may be asked to provide additional documentation during an audit. Fraudulent expense claims are treated as a serious violation and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
