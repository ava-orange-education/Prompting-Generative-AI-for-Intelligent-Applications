Employee Code of Conduct

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Department: Human Resources and Compliance

Purpose
This code of conduct outlines the professional and ethical standards expected of all employees. It applies to full-time employees, part-time employees, contractors, and interns. Adherence to this code is a condition of employment and is reviewed during annual performance evaluations.

Respect and Professionalism
All employees must treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and courtesy. Discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, disability, or any other characteristic is strictly prohibited. Harassment in any form, including verbal, physical, or digital, will not be tolerated. Employees who witness or experience inappropriate behavior should report it to HR immediately.

Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Any outside employment, consulting work, or business relationships that could create a conflict must be disclosed to the employee's manager and HR. Approval is required before engaging in any outside activity that may overlap with company business.

Confidentiality and Data Protection
Employees must protect all confidential company information, including financial data, customer records, trade secrets, and internal communications. Sharing confidential information outside the company without authorization is a serious violation. Employees must comply with all data protection regulations, including GDPR where applicable. When leaving the company, employees must return all company data and materials.

Use of Company Resources
Company resources, including laptops, software, office supplies, and internet access, are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable as long as it does not interfere with work responsibilities. Employees must not use company resources for illegal, unethical, or offensive activities. All software must be used in accordance with licensing agreements.

Attendance and Punctuality
Employees are expected to arrive on time and be present during scheduled working hours. Repeated lateness or unexplained absences may result in disciplinary action. If an employee is unable to attend work, they must inform their manager as early as possible. The leave policy provides full details on approved absence types and procedures.

Social Media and Public Statements
Employees must be careful when posting about the company on social media or in public forums. Personal opinions must not be presented as company positions. Employees must not share internal information, unreleased products, or confidential business details online. When in doubt about what is appropriate to share, consult the communications team.

Gifts and Entertainment
Employees may not accept gifts valued at more than 50 dollars from clients, vendors, or business partners. Small tokens such as branded merchandise or holiday cards are acceptable. Any gift or invitation that could appear to influence business decisions must be reported to the compliance team. Employees must not offer gifts to government officials or public servants.

Reporting Violations
Employees who become aware of any violation of this code must report it promptly. Reports can be made to the employee's manager, the HR department, or through the anonymous ethics hotline. The company prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith. All reports will be investigated and handled with appropriate confidentiality.

Disciplinary Action
Violations of this code of conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. The severity of the action will depend on the nature of the violation. Minor violations may result in a verbal or written warning. Serious violations, such as fraud, harassment, or data breaches, may lead to immediate termination and possible legal action.
