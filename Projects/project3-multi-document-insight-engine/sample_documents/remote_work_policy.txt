Remote Work Policy

Effective Date: March 1, 2025
Department: Human Resources

Eligibility for Remote Work
All full-time employees who have completed their probation period of 3 months are eligible to apply for remote work. Contractors and part-time employees are not eligible under this policy. Eligibility does not guarantee approval. Managers may assess suitability based on role requirements and team needs.

Approved Remote Work Schedule
Eligible employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week. The remaining 2 days must be spent in the office. Tuesdays and Thursdays are designated as mandatory in-office days for all teams. Employees may choose their remaining remote days in coordination with their manager.

Equipment and Workspace
The company will provide a laptop and headset for remote work. Employees are responsible for maintaining a reliable internet connection and a quiet, professional workspace. Any additional equipment requests will be reviewed by the IT department. The company does not reimburse home internet or electricity costs.

Communication Expectations
Remote employees must be available during core working hours from 10 AM to 4 PM in their local time zone. All team communication should happen through the company's approved tools, including Slack for messaging and Zoom for video calls. Employees must respond to messages within 1 hour during core hours. Camera should be turned on during team meetings unless there is a technical issue.

Performance and Accountability
Remote work does not change performance expectations. Employees are expected to meet the same deadlines, quality standards, and output levels as when working from the office. Managers will conduct monthly check-ins to review remote work effectiveness. If performance declines, the remote work arrangement may be revised or revoked.

Data Security and Confidentiality
Employees must follow all data security policies while working remotely. Company data must not be accessed from public networks without a VPN. Confidential documents must not be printed at home unless approved by the compliance team. Employees must lock their devices when stepping away from their workspace.

Professional Conduct
Remote employees are expected to maintain the same professional standards as in-office employees. This includes punctuality for meetings, respectful communication, and adherence to the company code of conduct. Casual attire is acceptable for internal meetings, but professional dress is required for client-facing calls.

Revocation of Remote Work Privileges
The company reserves the right to revoke remote work privileges at any time. Common reasons for revocation include repeated missed deadlines, failure to attend mandatory in-office days, or violations of the data security policy. Employees will receive a written notice before any change to their remote work arrangement.

Travel and Office Visits
Remote employees who live more than 50 kilometers from the office may be required to visit the office for quarterly team meetings. Travel costs for mandatory office visits will be reimbursed according to the company travel policy. Employees must submit travel expenses within 15 days of the visit.
